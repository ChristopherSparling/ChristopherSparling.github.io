Rubik's Cube Solver

Lego NXT Powered Cube Solver

Developed for both MTE100 and GENE121, the Rubik's Cube Solver was one of the 1A semester's cumulative projects. The system was programmed using the Robot C language (native to LEGO NXT systems) and the Pair Programming methodology. The cube is solved using the beginner method which can be found here.

All code was written by the group with no external sources outside of beginner method documentation. It was programmed to minimize the angular loss to the inconsitency of the LEGO NXT motors as well as constantly calibrated as the cube being used became looser. This was arguably the most difficult aspect of the project as it was a standard Rubik's Cube being used (not a speed cube) and was not designed for precise rotations. The low tolerance for error between rotations was something the group struggled with and ultimately overcame.

  • Date: September - December 2015
  • For: University of Waterloo
  • Collaborators: Brandon Berchtold, Reuben Breukelman, Pranav Barot

Fuel Cell Powered Car

Four Cell Hydrogen Powered Vehicle

Created for MTE100, CHE100, and GENE121, the Fuel Cell Car served as the second cumulative project for the 1A semester. The supporting software was written in C for the MSP430 microcontroller framework. Fuel cells were monitored and improved through data analysis and regular operation. The vehicle was programmed to autonomously follow drawn paths through the utilization of an integrated color sensor system.

The most challenging aspects of the project were the electrical and sensor issues. The vehicles had been fabricated in the early 2010's; age and repeated use had put them in poor condition prior to our group working on them. These were eventually overcome with some small soldering works and wire replacements, but it made the final several weeks hectic as new problems popped up.

  • Date: October - December 2015
  • For: University of Waterloo
  • Collaborators: Eric Tweedle, Mauris Winata

Redesign of RBS Filtration System

SR&ED Program Submission

The RBS Filtration System was a product offered by Concord Screen during its early years of operation but fell out of production due to unreliability and poor cleaning performance. I was tasked to redesign the system's filtrate removal mechanism (its main point of failure) to possibly revive the product. The project was created with the intention of ultimately submitting it to the SR&ED government program so that even if the redesign proved unfeasible, their would still be a net gain in the research conducted. Countless iterations were developed for the various parts of the system ranging from disc used to scrape the inside of the wedge-wire screen to the support system that would be used above and below the basket were an auger system implemented.

This project reached the prototype fabrication stage (see above image) in which a miniature, hand-cranked version of the auger would be tested as a POC. There were issues regarding the company's ability to fabricate the auger to the required circular tolerance (the auger would need to make contact with the wire basket at all points) which were left unaddressed in the report due to my co-op ending.
